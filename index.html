Martin Luther King Jr

A man who fought injustice and strived for all men to be equal

Here's a time line of Dr. King's life:

  • January 15, 1929 - Martin Luther King, Jr. is born in Atlanta, Georgia.
  • June 1948 - King graduates from Morehouse College in Atlanta.
  • September 1948 - King enrolls in Crozer Theological Seminary in Chester, Pennsylvania.
  • June 18, 1953 - King marries Coretta Scott in Marion, Alabama.
  • December 1955 - King is elected the president of the Montgomery Improvement Association.
  • January 30, 1956 - King's house is bombed.
  • January 1957 - King is elected president of the Southern Christian Leadership Conference.
  • August 28, 1963 - King gives his "I Have a Dream" speech.
  • December 10, 1964 - King wins the Nobel Peace Prize.
  • April 4, 1968 - King is assassinated in Memphis
  • November 2, 1986 - King's birthday is declared a national holiday.

"I refuse to accept the view that mankind is so tragically bound to the starless midnight of racism and war that the bright daybreak of peace and brotherhood can never become a reality... I believe that unarmed truth and unconditional love will have the final word."
